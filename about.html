What exactly is Swivro?

We are Swivro, the privacy brand, founded in 2016, creating and offering a variety of software centered around privacy, cybersecurity, transparency, and decentralization. No personalized advertisements, no tracking, no data mining, no BS. Unlike the majority of other "privacy-oriented" organizations, we're not here for the money. Based in Japan, we're trying to make the internet a more private, secure, easily-accessible, and decentralized place.

I originally created Swivro in 2016, for myself. It started with a privacy-respecting web browser for Microsoft Windows, programmed in C# (.NET) using the Gecko Browser Engine developed by Mozilla. Unfortunately I've lost the source code for that browser, but I do have the executable which I can probably decompile. In the future, this rather unique web browser may be continued. At the time of Swivro's creation and the creation of this C# web browser, I noticed that companies such as Google would collect an absurd amount of data from you, to serve you personalized advertisements. Users on the internet should have privacy, they shouldn't be tracked across the internet by tech giants.

Decentralization is important for numerous reasons. It's a great way to retain your digital privacy and anonymity. Only in the year 2020 did we discover the importance of decentralization. The majority of all online services & products are centralized. Some examples include Google Search Engine, Discord, Twitter, Coinbase, and more. In some cases, centralization can be beneficial. Here's an example. Lets take a normal domain name, example.com. This domain name is (indirectly) controlled by a centralized authority - ICANN. Now lets take a decentralized domain name hosted on the blockchain, example.crypto. If our normal domain name, example.com, gets compromised/hacked and is transferred to the hacker's domain account therefore taking control of the domain, we can contact ICANN (the centralized authority) and provide proof of ownership to get our domain name back. If our decentralized blockchain domain, example.crypto, gets compromised/hacked, you lost that domain name forever, because there is no centralized authority that you can contact to get your domain name back, or to report that it was hacked/compromised.

Now, don't let this be a put-off. Decentralization comes with many benefits. A decentralized service does not have a single point of failure, nor does it run off of one machine (or multiple machines in the same location). Instead, a decentralized service consists of many machines hosted by people across the globe, all working together, creating a decentralized network. This means, the service practically cannot be shut down, because to shut it down, you would need to shut down every individual machine/server across the globe, which is essentially impossible. This also means, such services are censorship-resistant. Protocols like Email are decentralized, because you can send an email from your Gmail (hosted on Google Servers) to another person using a different email provider like Outlook (using Microsoft Servers). You can't shut down Email because it has no single point of failure and isn't a service itself - you can think of it as a decentralized network of servers all designed to communicate with each other, having no single point of failure. This also means that Email is censorship-resistant. Decentralized services allow you to evade censorship, and retain your online privacy. This is why we do not want Swivro to ever be one giant entity offering many centralized services - we want Swivro to be one of many organizations offering similar privacy products & resources. Some more examples of decentralized services & software include Matrix (a modern decentralized communications software), Bitcoin, Monero, Ethereum, Decentrasearch (our product), and Mastodon (modern decentralized social media).

At the time of writing this, Swivro is hosted by Webdock, a server hosting company. Our website is open source, so you can fork our website's GitHub repository to make your own site based on Swivro, further decentralizing the internet.
