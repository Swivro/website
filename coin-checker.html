Scam - DO NOT BUY!

Not every memecoin is a complete scam!

The majority of what's called "memecoins" are scams, but not all of them are. Wownero (a software fork of Monero) is a great example. It's considered a "memecoin" as it has a meme-inspired name, however, the coin itself is not a scam, as they are very honest with their buyers and the public, on their website.

How do we determine whether or not a coin is a scam?

We, mostly, ask to ourselves, three questions about the coin in question, being (1) does the coin claim to be something it is not, (2) do the devs consistently overpromise and underdeliver, and (3) does the coin have a sensible whitepaper. Even if the coin has a sensible whitepaper, but consistently overpromises & underdelivers, and claims to be something it's not, it will be considered a scam.
