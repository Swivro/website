Which companies track me?

A list of some companies to avoid

All of the companies below violate your privacy in some way. Luckily, we provide recommendations to alternatives. See them here.

Google

AVOID

Obvious reasons. They unethically track you, sell your data, perform browser fingerprinting, and a bunch of other nasty privacy-compromising stuff. This includes all Google Services, not just Google Search.

Windows

AVOID

With the upcoming Windows 11 release, we are almost certain that it is full of spyware, why else would it be a free upgrade? Windows will likely collect data from you as you use your Windows 11 PC.

Apple

AVOID

We were never fans of Apple, but we accepted that they provided a relatively alright service. Apple has crossed the line with their new protocol and has removed end-to-end device encryption. Anyone can look at your photos now.

Yandex

AVOID

You would think Yandex doesn't unethically track you, maybe because they are not based in the USA. To summarize, Yandex is the Russian Google. They do just as many nasty things behind your back as Google does.

Facebook

AVOID

By using Facebook, you enable Facebook and 3rd party companies affiliated with Facebook to track your every move. Your private messages are absolutely not private (this is factual), and Zuckerberg is an idiot. Facebook also appears to have a bias, limiting free speech (no matter who you are).

Bytefend

AVOID

What seems to be a decent cybersecurity company turns out to be ran by a group of (kids?) people who threaten to launch DDoS attacks against practically anyone who looks at them funny. They also are very unprofessional, and protect illegal services via their cloud network.

Zoom

AVOID

Zoom, the extremely popular video conferencing app nowadays. They have been giving/selling (one of the two, it is not fully known) your data (including but not limited to video call recordings, chat messages, etc) unethically to 3rd party companies without notice.

WhatsApp

AVOID

WhatsApp is the very popular calling/texting app used by millions across the globe. While they technically have better security in a way than Telegram (WhatsApp provides e2ee by default while Telegram doesn't), they've been secretly sharing your data with Facebook as per their new terms and conditions.

Yahoo

AVOID

Yahoo, specifically the Email service, isn't really the worst Email service in the world. However, they have experienced and continue to experienced multiple data breaches. This makes their security and infrastructure questionable. Yahoo also (according to some reliable sources) scans every email sent and received for data to sell to advertisers.

Amazon & AWS

AVOID

Amazon, when it comes to privacy, is a steaming hot pile of garbage. Amazon owns Ring, the camera that you put in front of your house. There's no doubt that Amazon is constantly monitoring what's going on through your Ring Doorbell Camera. Additionally, Amazon & AWS likely sells your data behind your back.

Discord

AVOID

Discord tracks your IP address and in-app activity from when you log in until you log out. This data may be sold to 3rd parties. There is no way to confirm this, but Discord will not say anything 🤔. Discord may also unethically collect information from your connected social accounts.

GitHub

AVOID

There is no doubt that GitHub tracks you and collects information from you and your account, as GitHub is owned by Microsoft. Microsoft isn't entirely bad (privacy-wise), but when it comes to GitHub and Windows (both owned by Microsoft), they certainly track you.

Swivro FAQ

How long has Swivro been around?

Swivro was founded in the year 2016 under the name DiskNet. We've operated under a few other names, but we're now known as Swivro and have no plans to change our name any time soon.

Why should I trust you?

We take many precautions to ensure maximum security, privacy, and anonymity for our visitors. When possible, we store as little data as possible from you. You're welcome to access Swivro.Net via Tor without getting blocked.

Is Swivro Free?

"Swivro" itself is not a product, but it offers services, like Speaklink. Most of our products are free whilst some are considered a premium service, requiring a payment in order to use the service.

What do you develop/offer?

Swivro offers online privacy-focused products & services, with no unethical tracking involved whatsoever. Additionally, we also offer open source software available on via our Git organization.

Can I contribute to Swivro?

If you have a suggestion or concern, you can email us at any time via support@swivro.com
