Lack of transparency

Companies these days aren't very clear on what they are doing with info they secretly collect from you. We believe that's wrong.

Google, Microsoft, & more are not transparent with what's really going on.

Almost all tech giants you use for online services (Google, Microsoft, Yandex, Facebook, Twitter, Apple, and more) are not very transparent in regards to what's really going on behind your back, including selling your data, spying on you, collecting information from you, and more.

  • What is the issue?
  • Why should you care?
  • What is the solution?

A lot of companies on the internet simply lack transparency. Companies that are not transparent typically collect data about you and refuse to tell you what they are doing with that data, and exactly what data they have collected from you and retain in their database. 

Without company transparency, companies like Google, Microsoft, and more, can collect very sensitive information about you behind your back, and they can basically do what ever they want with it as long as it's not illegal. This includes sharing it with 3rd party companies, advertisers, Facebook, the government (the NSA, FBI, etc), and more. You should have the right to know what is going on with your data.

While there isn't a clear solution for you that we can put here, we suggest always using a VPN such as ProtonVPN or Mullvad, and you should always read the company's privacy policy. In the EU, privacy concerns were somewhat addressed in 2018 with the GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation). There are 99 articles in GDPR. These include the right to know what data a company holds about you, consent to collection of personal identifiable data, and much more.
