See the best alternatives to Google Search

By Albert A.
November 1, 2021

It's time to finally stop using Google, Bing, and Ecosia, because they all unethically track you and store sensitive information from you without consent. Discover search engines that doesn't track your search queries or display personalized ads based on your search history. Here are our favorites, listed below:

Searx is an open-source, self-hostable, metasearch engine, aggregating the results of other search engines while not storing information about its users. Searx is kind enough to provide a list of public instances, or you can try out the NixNet Public instance.

DuckDuckGo is a "search engine that doesn't track you." Some of DuckDuckGo's code is free software hosted at GitHub, but the DuckDuckGo core is unfortunatley proprietary, meaning we will not be able to see exactly what's going on. The company is based in the USA.

Qwant is a search engine with its philosophy based on two principles: no user tracking and no filter bubble.  The company is based in France. While the complete source code of Qwant is not publicly accessible, it is believed that Qwant does not unethically track you, does not sell your data, and does not collect and save your search queries for research purposes.

Startpage is a search engine that displays search results from Google with complete privacy protection against Google's nasty tracking software. Startpage BV is a Netherlands-based company that has been dedicated to privacy-respecting web search since 2006. While the complete source code to Startpage is not publicly accessible, it is believed that Startpage does not unethically track you, doesn't sell your data, and does not collect and save your search queries for research purposes. Warning: Startpage was recently acquired by System1, a US-based analytics company.

Mojeek is a search engine that you can really trust, based in the United Kingdom. The search results which are provided by Mojeek come from its own index of web pages (unlike Startpage and DuckDuckGo), created by crawling the web. Mojeek is completely open source, so you can see everything that is going on when you use Mojeek, by looking at it's source code. Mojeek is also the first search engine to have a policy of not tracking its users.

All of these search engines recommended above are unique in their own way. We suggest that you carefully reviewing the info we provided about them, and try each of them out individually to determine which one is best for you. Thanks for reading!
