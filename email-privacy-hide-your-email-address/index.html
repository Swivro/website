Hide your real email address from the public

By Albert A.
November 1, 2021

Publicly disclosing your email address is dangerous for many reasons with the biggest reason being; by disclosing your email address, it shows what email provider you use. For example, if my Email is example@gmail.com, and I publicly disclose that email, a hacker can go to gmail.com and try to hack into my account by cracking my password or via social engineering. Your email can also end up in a bad place (ex: dangerous dark web forums), which can lead to bad things. Below, we'll be suggesting two services which allow you to create email aliases, so you can still receive emails on your email address, but your real email address won't be public.

Option 1: AnonAddy

AnonAddy lets you create your own email aliases to mask your real email address. If someone asks for your email address and you don't fully trust them, you can login to your AnonAddy account, and create a new email alias that forwards all mail sent to the email alias to your real email address. If my email alias is bob@anonaddy.me and my real email address is bobsmith@gmail.com, all mail sent to bob@anonaddy.me will be anonymously forwarded to bobsmith@gmail.com.
 
TL;DR AnonAddy let's you receive emails without exposing your real email, by using email aliases. Get started for free at their website anonaddy.com
 

Option 2: SimpleLogin

As the name suggests, SimpleLogin is very simple. SimpleLogin essentially does the same thing as AnonAddy, but as mentioned, is more simple and straight to the point. SimpleLogin is 100% open source and uses PGP Encryption (as does AnonAddy). Lastly, SimpleLogin appears to have better pricing when compared to AnonAddy, but they are both good solutions, you'll just have to choose which one you like more.

TL;DR SimpleLogin does the same thing as AnonAddy but is simpler and has better pricing. Get started for free at their website simplelogin.io
