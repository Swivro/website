Encipher

Simply, securely, and privately encrypt text or the contents of a file. You can use Encipher for many things, such as; to encipher & decipher the contents of an email, to encipher the seed phrase to your crypto wallet, to encipher a self-note, SMS messages, & more.

Frequently asked questions

  • How can I trust this? Is this another crypto scam?
    You can see all of the code of this tool in your browser, by typing view-source: before the URL. The git repository is also available here -> https://github.com/Swivro/encipher
  • How do I know you don't log my passphrase?
    We don't log anything you type here. You can verify this by opening the developer tools in your browser and seeing if anything new pops up while typing a passphrase. So, if you don't trust this, you can self-host it, here's the code -> https://github.com/Swivro/encipher
  • Is all information sent across the internet encrypted?
    No information entered on this page is sent anywhere across the internet. Encryption and decryption takes place locally on your device/machine. No info ever leaves your machine.
  • Who made this application?
    Licensed under GNU General Public License v3.0, this application is a modified fork of zohar1000/in-browser-encryption. The source code for our fork is available at https://github.com/Swivro/encipher. Changes can be seen on our README file.
  • Where is my encrypted text and passphrase stored?
    We do not store either of those things. As mentioned, encryption & decryption takes place locally, in your browser. No information typed into this page ever reaches us or any 3rd party servers.
