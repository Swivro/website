Malicious Companies

The list below shows various companies that track you, don't care about security, or violate your online privacy. Avoid these companies.

What counts as a "malicious" company?

Malicious companies listed on this page are companies that lack one of many qualities (seen below). These companies don't care about your privacy, have bad security, or are known to be awful in one or more ways. A malicious company typically has one or more of the following traits:

  • Unethical Tracking
  • Selling your data
  • Bad Security
  • Multiple data breaches
  • A complete scam
  • Highly unreliable

Google

AVOID

Obvious reasons. They unethically track you, sell your data, perform browser fingerprinting, and a bunch of other nasty privacy-compromising stuff. This includes all Google Services, not just Google Search. There are plenty of alternatives to Google which can be found via a bit of research.

Comcast

AVOID

You may be using Comcast as your internet service provider depending on where you live. Comcast has never promised to sell your internet data, does not notify you if the government is requesting data related to you, and got a 1-star privacy rating from the Electric Frontier Foundation.

Apple

AVOID

We were never fans of Apple, but we accepted that they provided a relatively alright service. Apple has crossed the line with their new protocol and has removed end-to-end device encryption. Anyone from Apple can look at your saved photos now if they want to.

Yandex

AVOID

You would think Yandex doesn't unethically track you, maybe because they are not based in the USA, and because Yandex isn't directly affiliated with Google or Microsoft. To summarize, Yandex is the Russian Google. They do just as many nasty things behind your back as Google does.

Facebook

AVOID

By using Facebook, you enable Facebook and 3rd party companies affiliated with Facebook to track your every move. Your private messages are absolutely not private (this is factual), and Zuckerberg is an idiot in regards to everything. Facebook also appears to have a bias, limiting free speech (no matter who you are).

Zoom

AVOID

Zoom, it's the extremely popular video conferencing app nowadays. They have been giving/selling (one of the two, it is not fully known) your data (including but not limited to video call recordings, chat messages, etc) unethically to 3rd party companies without notifying you.

Discord

AVOID

Discord locks accounts that don't have associated phone numbers. This means Discord wants your personal phone number (VOIP not allowed) for some weird reason. Additionally, they refuse to publicly say whether they sell your data to 3rd party companies or not.

WhatsApp

AVOID

WhatsApp is the very popular calling/texting app used by millions across the globe. While they technically have better security in a way than Telegram (WhatsApp provides e2ee by default while Telegram doesn't), they've been secretly sharing your data with Facebook as per their new terms and conditions. If you didn't know, Facebook owns WhatsApp.

Instagram

AVOID

By using Instagram (owned by Facebook) you enable Facebook and 3rd party companies affiliated with Facebook to track your every move. Your private messages are absolutely not private (this is factual), and Zuckerberg is an idiot. Additionally there's no doubt that Instagram tracks your location and logs it.

Yahoo

AVOID

Yahoo, specifically the Email service, isn't really the worst Email service in the world. However, they have experienced and continue to experienced multiple data breaches. This makes their security and infrastructure questionable. Yahoo also (according to some reliable sources) scans every email sent and received for data to sell to advertisers.

Amazon & AWS

AVOID

Amazon, when it comes to privacy, is a steaming hot pile of garbage. Amazon owns Ring, the camera that you put in front of your house. There's no doubt that Amazon is constantly monitoring what's going on through your Ring Doorbell Camera. Additionally, Amazon & AWS likely sells your data behind your back.

GitHub

AVOID

There is no doubt that GitHub tracks you and collects information from you and your account, as GitHub is owned by Microsoft. Microsoft isn't entirely bad (privacy-wise), but when it comes to GitHub and Windows (both owned by Microsoft), they certainly track you.

HostGator

AVOID

HostGator uses Facebook, Google Ads, Google Analytics, Hotjar, and Microsoft to collect your data and to likely sell it. HostGator couldn't care less about your privacy. They also love to try and upsell you, by showing fake web hosting deals, like "USE COUPON SNAPPY TO GET 50% OFF, OFFER ENDS SOON!" while that coupon has always and will always exist.

Epik

AVOID

We've heard from sources that Epik hired some developers to develop their system, who used MD5 without salt. Anonymous recently hacked into Epik, leaking hundreds of gigabytes of data from thousands of hosted websites, credit card information, physical addresses, domain transfer EPP codes, and more. Additionally, they claim to be free-speech, but you'll get kicked off if what you contradicts what Rob (Epik CEO) believes.

GoDaddy

AVOID

Like HostGator, GoDaddy doesn't give two sh**s about your privacy. They use Amazon, Microsoft, Facebook, Google, Pinterest, Chinese TikTok, and more to collect and likely sell your data. GoDaddy is also overpriced, tries to upsell you, and provides a very sh**ty service. They are incredibly overpriced and charge you $14.99 per year for WHOIS Privacy, while nearly every other registrar provides it for free.

Hola! VPN

AVOID

Hola! VPN is known to be one of the worst VPN providers in existence. They don't care about user privacy, probably retain user logs, and in the year 2015, Hola! VPN was caught turning their users' devices/computers into Exit Nodes, along with unethically selling bandwidth to third parties. Never consider using Hola! VPN.

Opera Browser

AVOID

The popular browser called Opera now includes a built-in VPN by default. Despite it being called a VPN, it is not a VPN at all, it's simply a web proxy that prevents websites you visit through Opera from seeing your real home IP address, but that's not the bad part. This free included Chinese "VPN" unethically collects data about the browser user (you) and shares it with 3rd parties.

Parler

AVOID

In 2021, Parler suffered one of the biggest data breaches of the year. Over 70 terabytes of data was scraped, including sensitive user information. This data breach exposed over 99% of the app's private messages, posts, drivers license's, and more. This "hack" was considered legal, because it was possible due to a flaw in Parler's API which allowed anyone to download this sensitive information.

AT&T

AVOID

In early 2006, Electric Frontier Foundation obtained whistleblower evidence from former AT&T technician Mark Klein showing that AT&T is cooperating with illegal surveillance. The undisputed documents show that AT&T installed a fiberoptic splitter at its facility in San Francisco that makes copies of all emails web browsing and other Internet traffic to and from AT&T customers and provides those copies to the NSA.

HideMyAss VPN

AVOID

In 2011, the FBI tracked a hacker's activities back to an IP address belonging to the HideMyAss VPN service. The FBI acquired activity logs from HideMyAss and used them to catch and prosecute the hacker. Despite the illegality of the hacker's wrongful actions, this incident made two things clear: HideMyAss does keep traceable logs and does not care so much about your privacy.
