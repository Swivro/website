Lack of Online Privacy

Most websites that you visit, software that you use, or internet providers that you use, simply do not value your online privacy.

What's the problem?

Your smartphone is incredibly useful, Google is extremely helpful, and your internet service provider is vital, but unfortunately they all spy on you and watch your every move. These companies sell your data, share it with 3rd parties, and store all of the information they have about you to track you across the internet.

Why you should care

You shouldn't confuse privacy with secrecy. We know what happens in the bathroom, but you still close the door. That's because you want privacy, not secrecy. Everyone has something to hide, privacy is something that makes you human. Imagine having surveillance cameras placed in your kitchen, bathroom, bedroom, and vehicle/car. You probably won't be too happy with that, which is why you shouldn't be happy about all of these companies spying on you online.

What's the solution?

Unfortunately, you will never be able to be 100% private online. You can get really close, but never perfect. When browsing the internet, or using the internet at all, we recommend using a VPN or the Tor Network (makes you way more anonymous). See our recommendations for VPN's and info about Tor on this page. Try to also avoid companies like Google, and to use more privacy-focused services, for example, instead of Google I can use Qwant or DuckDuckGo (which are both very privacy-respecting).
