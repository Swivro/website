Lack of Privacy

Most websites that you visit, software that you use, or internet providers that you use, simply do not value your online privacy.

Everyone deserves online privacy while using the internet. Including you.

Ever since 9/11 occurred, online privacy has been a bigger concern, as tech giants like Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and more, all unethically track you, spy on you, collect your data without consent, and, to put it simply, could not care less about you data. The Government included.

  • What is the issue?
  • Why should you care?
  • What is the solution?

Your smartphone is incredibly useful, Google is extremely helpful, and your internet service provider is vital, but unfortunately they all spy on you and watch your every move. These companies sell your data, share it with 3rd parties, and store all of the information they have about you to track you across the internet. 

You shouldn't confuse privacy with secrecy. We know what happens in the bathroom, but you still close the door. That's because you want privacy, not secrecy. Everyone has something to hide, privacy is something that makes you human. Imagine having surveillance cameras placed all around your home & inside every room of your home. You wouldn't like that.

Unfortunately, you will never be able to be 100% anonymous online. You can get really close, but never perfect. When browsing the internet, or using the internet at all, we recommend using a VPN in conjunction with Tor Browser. See our recommendations for VPN's and info about Tor on this page. Try to also tech giants like Google, and to use more privacy-focused services, for example, instead of Google Search, I can use DuckDuckGo.
