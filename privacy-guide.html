A quick privacy guide

Stop using privacy-violating tech giants and discover privacy-first alternatives, including Email Providers, Messengers, and more.

If you want to stay safe online, you should keep your online privacy & stay anonymous.

Chances are, you're probably using a product owned and managed by a tech giant such as Google, Microsoft, Apple, or more. Obviously, as you know, none of these companies are very friendly towards user privacy. Below, we'll list alternatives to products owned by these privacy-violating tech giants.

Communications  (texting, calling, etc)

Session   Element   Status   Signal   IRC  

Email Provider  (like Gmail, Yahoo, etc)

ProtonMail   StartMail   Tutanota   Disroot   Mailfence  

Email Forwarding  (mask your email address)

AnonAddy   SimpleLogin  

Social Media  (microblogging aka like twitter)

Mastodon   Diaspora   Gab Social  

Operating System  (kinda like windows)

Ubuntu     Zorin OS     DraugerOS   Tails OS  

Browser  (stop using Chrome already)

Tor   Firefox   Waterfox  

Password Manager  (stop trying to remember your passwords)

Levin   LessPass   Bitwarden  

VPN Software  (hide & protect your public IP Address)

ProtonVPN   Mullvad   Njalla-VPN  

DNS Resolvers  (alternatives to Google DNS)

AdGuard   BlahDNS   1.1.1.1   LibreDNS   NextDNS   Quad9  

Hosting Providers  (similar to GoDaddy/HostGator)

Webdock   Njalla   Bahnhof   1984 Hosting  

Cloud File Storage  (like Google Drive & Dropbox)

Nextcloud   Proton Drive   Sync  

Spreadsheets/Doc Editing  (sort of like microsoft office & google docs)

LibreOffice   CryptPad  

Search Engine  (google/bing alternatives)

DuckDuckGo   Qwant   Startpage   Mojeek   Searx  

Crypto Exchanges  (exchange fiat for crypto, vice-versa, or crypto for crypto)

Binance   Bisq   Swapzone (crypto 4 crypto)
