ProtonVPN caught requesting personal information

By Albert A.
November 3, 2021

We are truly disappointed in Proton AG, the company that owns the popular ProtonVPN and ProtonMail. We are actually customers of ProtonVPN and ProtonMail, but after this disappointing and horrible news, we'll be migrating away from them before the end of this year. Proton AG claims to care about user privacy, and that their VPN and Email service is heavily focused on that, but this most recent news says otherwise. A customer of Njalla, a free-speech and privacy-oriented domain registrar and hosting company, was hosting a website that stated some clear issues with ProtonVPN and ProtonMail. Rather than contacting the owner of that website to discuss the content posted there about Proton AG's products, a Proton AG legal counsel contacted Njalla (the domain registrar & hosting company). Proton AG demanded all available information regarding the author of the involved website rather than trying to work things out in a friendly manner. While we don't have the screenshots of Proton's exact message, Njalla's response can be seen below.

We're on Njalla's side here. Proton is in the wrong, and it's just very disappointing seeing a "privacy-focused" VPN & Email company demand private & sensitive user information without a warrant of any sort. Proton may be right about the post being incorrect (on the associated website), but that does not give them the right to demand private user information, and this post that Proton is complaining about was on a private blog. After this incident, many users have cancelled their subscriptions, including us, and we hope you can consider doing so too. Until Proton properly addresses this situation and apologizes, we strongly advise that you avoid any services from Proton AG (ProtonMail, ProtonVPN, ProtonDrive, etc), because clearly this company does not care about privacy as everyone initially thought. If you're seeking a ProtonVPN alternative, we recommend checking out Mullvad, and if you are looking for a ProtonMail alternative, we suggest having a look at Tutanota.

Proton, if you're reading this and have a problem with this post, send us an email at 6j8lizy3@anonaddy.me and don't send a damn legal counsel to our domain registrar.

TL;DR (too long, didn't read) Proton AG, the company that owns ProtonVPN and ProtonMail, sent a message to the domain registrar, Njalla, of a private blog, demanding that Njalla sends them all of the personal details of the domain registrant.
