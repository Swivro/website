Safe Hosting Providers

Most server hosting providers are not privacy-respecting, and are willing to take down your website due to them being anti-free-speech.

Looking for a secure hosting provider?

Chances are, you're currently using a hosting provider that has a political bias, is anti-free-speech, does not respect your privacy, and will give in to government requests. We're here to recommend you some European free-speech secure hosting providers that respect your privacy and won't give in to government requests. USA Hosting providers usually always give in to government requests.

Option 1: njalla

njalla was founded in the year 2017. njalla is privacy-friendly and free-speech. As njalla says, "When you buy a domain in our system, we're actually purchasing it for ourselves. We will be the actual owners of the domain, however, you will still have the full control over the domain name. You can either use our information, our nameservers or you can go with your own. If you ever want to move the domain from our system, we'll of course give you the domain without any additional costs". Their goal is to minimize your visibility (personal details) to the public. As for VPS/Server Hosting, they provide secure servers at a low cost. njalla also accepts cryptocurrency for increased anonymity.

Option 2: Bahnhof

Based in Sweden, Bahnhof provides low-cost virtual private servers. Bahnhof is privacy-focused, free-speech, and accepts various cryptocurrencies. Like njalla, they don't give into government requests easily, and they are not based in the USA. Server providers based in the USA are likely never good for privacy or free-speech, and will give in to government requests in an instant, as we saw with HMA VPN (see more info about HMA VPN's willingness to deface their customers on our malicious company directory page).

Option 3: 1984 Hosting

1984 Hosting (founded in 2006... yeah) offers privacy-focused VPS Hosting, with the option to install preconfigured OpenVPN, with all logs immediately going to /dev/null and using itself for DNS (a truly private VPN). As 1984 Hosting says, the only true non-logging fully private VPN is the one you have full control over. While they do offer VPS's, they also offer Shared Web Hosting and domain names, but we still recommend njalla for domain names, as they seem to be more focused on privacy-first domain registration.
