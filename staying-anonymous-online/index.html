Staying Anonymous Online

By Albert A.
October 31, 2021

Whether you like it or not, your ISP is very likely spying on your every move on the internet. Most notably; Comcast, AT&T, T-Mobile, and Verizon. We're going to help you stay anonymous and private, so your ISP won't be able to spy on you for any longer.

Option 1: Use the Tor Browser

Tor is a network of virtual tunnels that allows you to improve your privacy and security on the Internet. Tor works by sending your web traffic through three random servers (also known as Tor relays) in the Tor network. The last relay in the circuit (the “exit relay” or "exit node") then sends the traffic out onto the public internet. You can access websites via the Tor Network by downloading the Tor Browser. Your ISP will only be able to see that you hitting a Tor Node, and nothing else. A VPN in conjunction with Tor is recommended; we recommend trying ProtonVPN or Mullvad. If you use Tor without a VPN, your ISP will see that you're hitting a Tor Node. This can give them clues to something or can flag you as high risk.

 
 
Option 2: Use a standalone VPN

If you do not want to use the Tor Browser, which relays your web traffic through multiple different servers (making you anonymous) which can slow down a website's load-time a bit, you may use a standalone VPN without Tor. Using a VPN with Tor most definitely makes you more anonymous, but if your goal is to only stop your ISP from seeing what you're doing, and to hide your home IP address from websites, a standalone VPN is indeed a good idea. We recommend trying ProtonVPN or Mullvad. Please ensure that your DNS resolvers on your device are not set to your ISP's DNS resolvers. If they are, change them to AdGuard DNS, BlahDNS, or Quad9 DNS.

 
 
Option 3: Route all internet traffic through Tor

If you're a high threat level that is in need of all internet traffic to be routed through the Tor Network, we recommend using Tails OS. This OS is a Linux Distro, based off of Debian. It routes all traffic through the Tor Network, and blocks all traffic that does not go through Tor (both in/out). As you may have guessed, Tails OS is focused on privacy and anonymity. If you are using Microsoft Windows, and for some reason cannot switch to Linux, you can either try Torrifier or Tallow (which both route all traffic through Tor), or you can create a Virtual Machine on your machine via VirtualBox, install Tails OS on it, and use that VM for accessing the internet.
