Hide your web activity

Your ISP most likely spies on you, whether it's Verizon, AT&T, or Comcast/Xfinity. We're here to help you stay anonymous.

How can you stop your ISP from spying on you?

Whether you like it or not, your ISP is very likely spying on your every move on the internet. Specifically; Comcast, AT&T, T-Mobile, and Verizon, which are also all listed on our Malicious Company Directory. We're going to help you stay anonymous and private, so your ISP won't be able to spy on you for any longer.

Tor Network

Tor is a network of virtual tunnels that allows you to improve your privacy and security on the Internet. Tor works by sending your web traffic through three random servers (also known as Tor relays) in the Tor network. The last relay in the circuit (the “exit relay” or "exit node") then sends the traffic out onto the public internet. You can access websites via the Tor Network by downloading the Tor Browser. Your ISP will only be able to see that you hitting a Tor Node, and nothing else. A VPN in conjunction with Tor is recommended; we recommend trying ProtonVPN or Mullvad.

A standalone VPN

If you do not want to use the Tor Browser, which relays your web traffic through multiple different servers (making you anonymous) which can slow down a website's load-time a bit, you may use a standalone VPN without Tor. Using a VPN with Tor most definitely makes you more anonymous, but if your goal is to only stop your ISP from seeing what you're doing, and to hide your home IP address from websites, a standalone VPN is indeed a good idea. We recommend trying ProtonVPN or Mullvad. Please ensure that your DNS resolvers on your device are not set to your ISP's DNS resolvers. If they are, change them to AdGuard DNS, BlahDNS, or Quad9 DNS.
