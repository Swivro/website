The best ways to purchase cryptocurrency

By Dmitri
October 31, 2021

Cryptocurrency is very popular these days. Now, the question is, how do you get cryptocurrency? Well, there are a few options, and most of the popular options have a KYC (know your customer) policy, meaning you'll need to provide your ID and the last 4 digits of your Social Security Number. You can either exchange with a company (you pay with fiat currency/USD and you receive cryptocurrency), or you'll have to find someone to trade with on the internet that already has cryptocurrency, and trust that they wont scam you.

If you are willing to exchange with a company (you must provide your ID & SSN), we highly recommend Coinbase. It is very user-friendly, fast, and simple. Binance is a very similar platform, but we have some slight concerns with their friendliness towards user privacy, which is why we will not be recommending Binance. If you would like to anonymously buy and sell Bitcoin (or other cryptocurrencies), we recommend trying out Bisq; no registration required, easy to use. Bisq is open source, and utilizes the Tor Network to give their users maximum anonymity. If you already have cryptocurrency, but would like to exchange it for a different cryptocurrency (say you have Bitcoin but want to exchange to get Ethereum), we recommend having a look at Swapzone. We recently used Swapzone to exchange our Litecoin for Monero, and the process was seamless. No user registration is required, simply type in what you want to exchange and the amount you want to exchange, enter your email address, and boom, you can exchange, no ID verification required. The users on Swapzone are very trustworthy, and scamming would only hurt their reputation.

If you're looking for a central wallet for all of your cryptocurrencies, we suggest trying out Exodus. It's completely free and private, and is very user friendly. Exodus simplifies sending and receiving cryptocurrency. Exodus also has an exchange feature where you can exchange X cryptocurrency for Y cryptocurrency, but it does have high(er) fees and limits. If you're using Coinbase, then you likely don't need Exodus, as all of your cryptocurrency is already stored in your Coinbase account.

Cryptocurrency is a very good thing for many reasons; including user privacy, anonymity, sending money internationally, and much more.
