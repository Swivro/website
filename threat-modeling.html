1

First, what exactly is a threat model?

A threat model is a list of significant potential threats to your online privacy, online security, and/or anonymity. It's impossible to protect yourself from every online attacker, so you should focus on the most probable threats.
2

Now, what are some examples?

The average person's threat model may be [hiding their data from] large tech corporations such as Google, Apple, etc. An investigative journalist's threat model may be [protecting against] a foreign government.
3

Creating your threat model

Get started simply by answering the two following questions. What do you want to protect, and who do you want to protect it from? Maybe you want to protect your data from Google and other tech giants.
4

It's time to get started

Now that you have created your threat model, you should start protecting x from y. We offer a directory of privacy-focused services and software, as well as a Privacy Checklist which can help you get started.
