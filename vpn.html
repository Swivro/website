Virtual Private Network

Swivro VPN is a virtual private network that hides your IP address, protects you against certain types of cybercrime, and blocks ads.

Why do you need a VPN? What even is a VPN?

A VPN, short for Virtual Private Network gives you online privacy and helps keep you anonymous by hiding your home IP address. VPN Tunnels are encrypted making it so hackers cannot see any data that goes from your home network to the VPN server. Swivro VPN is not about unblocking country-restricted content, but is about privacy and security, by hiding your IP.

  • WireGuard Protocol
  • No long-term retained logs
  • Cross-Compatible
  • Dedicated IP Address
  • Full Encryption
  • Ad-Blocker integrated (DNS)

FAQ

Have questions? We have answers.

What are the monthly bandwidth limits?

Swivro VPN users do not have a set limit of how much bandwidth they are allowed to use per month. There is no monthly bandwidth quota. However, you are only allowed to use 12.5 MBPS 24/7, though you can burst to speeds of up to 1 GBPS for hours without issue or limitation.

Is DDoS Protection Included?

Rather than having all of our VPN users on one DDoS protected IP address, we're taking a different approach. Each VPN user has a dedicated IP address (without DDoS protection), meaning no one will be able to find and send a DDoS attack to your IP address unless you give away your IP address and specifically ask for it to be attacked, which is strictly prohibited.

Why cant I switch locations with my VPN?

Your VPN account has a dedicated IP address. IP Addresses cost us money, and if you were paying $6/month, we would be losing money by paying for a dedicated IP address for you in every location. Each VPN location cost $6/month individually. Swivro VPN has never been and will never be a VPN focused on unblocking country-restricted content, it is focused on online privacy and keeping you safe & anonymous online.

VPN Speed may vary

Your VPN speed may vary depending on multiple conditions. Your VPN will most certainly have a speed (worst case scenario) of 5 MBPS, but is capable of up to more than 800 MBPS on servers that have a network speed of 1 GBPS. Please note that this is the bandwidth which is "allocated" to each VPN account/tunnel: the bandwidth you can obtain depends also on your provider conditions, peering and several further conditions. To test your home network speed (without VPN), we recommend visiting speedtest.net
